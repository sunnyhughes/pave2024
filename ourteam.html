Who We Are

During the year 2021, a collective of parents and individuals deeply invested in social services collaborated to formulate a strategy aimed at mitigating the prevalence of violence among our youth and young adults. P.A.V.E. operates under the fiscal sponsorship of the Louisville Central Community Center, conducting weekly sessions at the Baptist Fellowship Center (BFC) in Louisville, Kentucky.

Our Team

The leadership team at P.A.V.E. consists of seven (7) dedicated individuals with extensive expertise in youth program development and implementation. Consisting of both active and retired professionals, such as teachers, school administrators, and individuals experienced in community engagement and development, the team is fervently dedicated to improving health outcomes for children, youth, and young adults, as well as their families and communities.

This diverse leadership brings a wealth of knowledge encompassing best practices in program development. Additionally, they possess a profound understanding of the cultural and social disparities that significantly influence health outcomes for individuals, families, and communities. Furthermore, their collective experience extends to a keen awareness of organizational structures and the implementation of efficient administrative practices.

Through their combined skills and commitment, P.A.V.E.'s leadership is well-equipped to drive positive change and contribute to the well being of the communities they serve.
